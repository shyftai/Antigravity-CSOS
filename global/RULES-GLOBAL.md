# Customer Success Rules — Global

These rules apply to ALL workspaces. They cannot be overridden.

## Health score standards

1. **Health scores are mandatory** — never take action on an account without checking its current health score. No exceptions.
2. **Honest scoring** — health scores reflect reality, not optimism. If usage is declining, the score reflects it. Never inflate a score to avoid a difficult conversation.
3. **Threshold enforcement** — red accounts (score < 40) get immediate attention. Amber accounts (40-69) get proactive outreach within 48 hours. Green accounts (70+) follow standard cadence.
4. **Trend over snapshot** — a single data point is noise. Health scores must consider 30-day and 90-day trends, not just today's numbers.

## Escalation SLAs

1. **Critical escalation** — executive sponsor involved, churn imminent: respond within 2 hours, action plan within 24 hours.
2. **High escalation** — product blocker, significant dissatisfaction: respond within 4 hours, action plan within 48 hours.
3. **Medium escalation** — feature gap, process issue: respond within 24 hours, resolution within 1 week.
4. **Low escalation** — minor inconvenience, enhancement request: acknowledge within 48 hours, route to backlog.
5. **Document everything** — every escalation gets logged with date, customer, severity, description, owner, and resolution.

## Communication standards

1. **Proactive over reactive** — customers should hear about issues from us before they discover them. If a release has a known issue, communicate it before support tickets arrive.
2. **No jargon in customer-facing content** — QBR decks, reports, and emails use plain language. Internal shorthand stays internal.
3. **Consistent cadence** — check-in frequency matches the customer's tier and health. Never go dark on an account.
4. **Executive alignment** — for enterprise accounts, ensure executive sponsors are included in quarterly communications at minimum.
5. **Tone** — professional, warm, direct. Never defensive. Never dismissive. Always solution-oriented.

## Data hygiene

1. **Timeline completeness** — every customer interaction (call, email, meeting, support ticket) gets logged in the timeline. No dark accounts.
2. **Contact accuracy** — verify key stakeholders quarterly. Champions leave, sponsors change roles. Stale contacts are a churn risk.
3. **Renewal dates** — renewal dates must be accurate and tracked. Discovering a renewal 2 weeks out is a failure. 90-day advance tracking minimum.
4. **No PII exposure** — customer data stays in its workspace. Never share customer information across workspaces or externally without explicit authorization.

## Churn response time

1. **Churn signal detected** — investigate within 24 hours. No churn signal sits unaddressed for more than one business day.
2. **Save attempt** — if a customer indicates intent to churn, a save plan must be in place within 48 hours. Escalate to leadership if needed.
3. **Post-churn debrief** — every churned customer gets a post-mortem within 1 week. Document root cause, what was tried, what would have helped, and feed into LEARNINGS.md.
4. **Win-back window** — churned customers enter a 90-day win-back track. After 90 days, archive. Never pester beyond the window.

## QBR cadence

1. **Enterprise** — quarterly QBRs minimum. Monthly check-ins. Executive sponsor briefing at least twice per year.
2. **Mid-market** — quarterly or semi-annual QBRs based on contract value. Monthly check-ins.
3. **SMB** — semi-annual business reviews or automated health check emails. Quarterly check-ins.
4. **At-risk override** — any account flagged red gets a QBR or executive check-in regardless of segment cadence.

## Financial integrity

1. **Transparent pricing** — all pricing changes, discounts, and contract modifications must be documented and approved before communicating to the customer.
2. **No unauthorized discounts** — discounts and concessions require explicit approval. Never promise a discount without authorization.
3. **Revenue accuracy** — ARR, MRR, expansion, and contraction figures must match billing system records. Never estimate when actuals are available.
