## Check-in emails

**Goal:** Maintain relationship, surface issues early, show you're paying attention.

**We sound like:**
- "I noticed your team's usage of [feature] picked up this month — great to see. Anything I can help optimize?"
- "Quick check-in ahead of your renewal. I've pulled together some usage highlights — let me know a good time to walk through them."

**We never sound like:**
- "Just checking in!" (empty, no value)
- "Per our agreement, your renewal is approaching..." (robotic, contract-focused)

**Rules:**
- Always reference something specific (usage data, recent conversation, support ticket)
- Keep to 3-5 sentences unless sharing a resource
- End with a clear next step, not an open question

## QBR presentations

**Goal:** Demonstrate value, align on goals, identify expansion.

**We sound like:**
- "Here's what your team accomplished this quarter, and where I see the biggest opportunity for next quarter."
- "Based on your goal of [outcome], here's how the data shows you're tracking."

**We never sound like:**
- "Here's a list of features we released." (product update, not QBR)
- "Your usage is down — what happened?" (accusatory)

**Rules:**
- Lead with customer outcomes, not product features
- Include specific metrics tied to their success plan
- End with 2-3 recommended next steps, not a feature dump
- Max 12 slides, 80% customer data / 20% product updates

## At-risk outreach

**Goal:** Re-engage without panic, diagnose before prescribing.

**We sound like:**
- "I wanted to reach out because I noticed [specific signal]. I want to make sure we're aligned on how to get the most value from [product]."
- "I know things have been busy — I'd love 15 minutes to understand what's working and what isn't."

**We never sound like:**
- "We noticed you haven't logged in..." (surveillance tone)
- "We'd hate to lose you as a customer." (desperate)
- "Is everything okay?" (vague, puts burden on customer)

**Rules:**
- Name the signal that triggered outreach (be transparent)
- Offer a specific, low-effort next step (15-min call, not a workshop)
- No guilt, no pressure, no urgency language
- If no response after 3 attempts, escalate internally — don't keep emailing

## Expansion conversations

**Goal:** Help customers get more value, not sell more product.

**We sound like:**
- "Based on how your team is using [feature], there's a natural fit for [additional capability]. Want me to show you what that would look like?"
- "A few customers in your space have seen strong results by adding [module]. Happy to share a case study if useful."

**We never sound like:**
- "Have you considered upgrading?" (salesy)
- "You're leaving money on the table." (presumptuous)

**Rules:**
- Expansion should feel like a recommendation, not a pitch
- Always tie to their stated goals or usage patterns
- Include proof (case study, benchmark, their own data)
- If they say no, document it and revisit in 90 days

## Support responses

**Goal:** Resolve quickly, maintain trust, keep CSM informed.

**We sound like:**
- "Thanks for flagging this. I've looped in our support team and I'll follow up once we have an update."
- "I looked into this — here's what's happening and what we're doing to fix it."

**We never sound like:**
- "That's a support issue, please submit a ticket." (deflecting)
- "I'll look into it." (vague, no timeline)

**Rules:**
- Acknowledge the issue immediately, even if the fix takes time
- Give a timeline for next update (even if it's "I'll update you by EOD tomorrow")
- If support is handling, CSM stays in the loop and follows up
- Never blame the customer or their configuration

---

## Banned phrases
- "Just checking in" (without context)
- "Per my last email"
- "As previously mentioned"
- "We'd hate to lose you"
- "That's not my area"
- "You should have..."
- "No worries" (after a real issue)
